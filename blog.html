Development and Planning
05.28.11

This Region has abundant natural beauty and a idyllic rural lifestyle. Yes we need business and employment, but we need appropriate commercial and industrial growth. Developments that “fit” with the character of the Region. Local and Sate Planning needs to recognise and support that. With regards to planning and development it was very informative to attend the Scenic Rim Sustainable Development Group meeting at Rosevale Frdiay the 12th Feb 2016. This was a meeting to discuss the Mt Walker Quarry and the Moorang “mega” chicken sheds developments. Both of these developments are inappropriate activities for the rural environment of the western area of the Scenic Rim Region. The quarry at Mt Walker was approved by the Mayor and his faction of Councillors on the basis it was too hard to oppose the developers who would take them to Court. There are perfectly valid reasons to reject the DA for several issues. These include no water on site to control dust, unsuitable roads for the 90-200 trucks per day, impact on neighbouring residents, lack of studies done to assess koala habitat and other potential threatened species in this area which still contains a large amount of remnant native vegetation. There is adequate stockpiles of basalt gravel, and there is no employment or other benefits flowing to the local economy. In regards to the proposed chicken sheds- there is every reason to “re-think” the whole chicken industry direction. The growing sheds and the processing facilities should all be located close to the food source – the Darling Downs. This would mean less trucks on the roads, with only the finished product being transported either by road, rail or air freight (Toowoomba Airport). This is a common sense and efficient use of land and freighting. Time this was seriously put on the table, and end the on-going battles which communities have to engage in to stop the sheds being placed in the Scenic Rim.

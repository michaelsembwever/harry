Welcome Message

My decision to run for Mayor is all about making a difference to the future direction of our beautiful region, to protect the history, the natural environment and our productive farmlands. Looking to the future we need a Council administration that is streamlined and is held accountable to a high performance standard and good governance. The Council managers and workforce need an environment in which they are given appropriate responsibilities and respect to do their jobs to the best of their abilities. Creating a sustainable future for our region is about having a Mayor and Council which actively seek new opportunities , encourage new business which give the region long term employment growth while protecting and enhancing the unique qualities of our region.

Biography

I am a 61 year old Veterinary Surgeon. Born in Sydney, NSW, raised as a child in Hong Kong Japan and Australia. My parents are Dutch, and are now in their 90s and living in NSW. My veterinary studies were at Sydney University, and I also did post graduate diplomas in Natural Resources and Dispute Resolution. I have two adult children and 3 granddaughters who have recently moved from Norway where they were born. I live in an old historic church in Milford just outside Boonah which I run as a bed and breakfast accommodation and small function venue.

I have worked in the veterinary industry for 38 years, mostly in small and large animal practice. In that time I have established three seperate Veterinary practices in Sydney, in Bowral (NSW) and more recently in Boonah (Walter St Veterinary Clinic). In the 1990s I spent 5 years working for the NSW Veterinary Surgeons Board as their veterinary hospital inspector and complaints officer.                                                                                    

During my time working as a veterinary surgeon I pursued other interests including wildlife management, environmental studies, and working with both the Australian Veterinary Association and Wingecarribee Shire Council. I have also taught Environmental Studies for NSW TAFE including doing bushwalking excursions.

My interest in local government includes working with Council and working for the community in campaigns to save prime agricultural land in the Wingecarribee Shire I established one of Australia's first non euthanasia animal shelters in Wingecarribee Shire. I studied Natural Resources at UNE where I did subjects in Town Planning and Land Use Management. My interests in wildlife management led to me to realise that to protect and manage our wildlife we need to stop loss of habitat and therefore manage urban and commercial developments to minimize damage to wildlife habitat and corridors. I was a founding member of WIRES which was a pioneer in creating a statewide wildlife rescue network.

 

Hobbies and Interests

I participate actively in cycling, swimming and triathlons. My more sedate interests are gardening and renovating, I have renovated four houses as an owner builder, and created many gardens with an eye to a mix of native gardens and cottage garden. I am a member of the Scenic Rim Tourism Inc, the Scenic Rim Cycling Group, BOSS, Scenic Rim Ratepayers Association, and the Botanical Gardens. I  am a keen bushwalker and  bird enthusiast, and enjoy  travelling to interesting destinations.
